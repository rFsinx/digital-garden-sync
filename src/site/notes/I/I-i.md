# Arithmetics

### addition model (shift)
---
$$ x \xrightarrow{+n} x+n \quad \text{translation by } n \text{ units} $$
**subtraction (reverse process)**
$$ x+n \xrightarrow{-n} x $$
**subtraction is addition** 
$$ x+n+(-n) = x $$
### multiplication model (scaling)
---
$$ x \xrightarrow{\times n} x \times n \quad \text{radial amplification of distance from 0}$$
**division (reverse process)**
$$ x \times n \xrightarrow{\div n} x \quad (n \neq 0) $$
**division is multiplication**
$$ x \div n \quad \text{and} \quad x \times \frac{1}{n} \quad \text{are equal statements}$$

# Algebraic Axioms

a, b, c are all numbers in the number line

### commutative law
---
$$ a+b = b+a \qquad a \times b = b \times a$$
### associative law 
---
$$ (a+b)+c = a+(b+c) \qquad (a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c) $$
### distributive law
---
$$ a \times (b + c) = a \times b + a \times c $$
### use
---
$$
\begin{align}
& a \times 0 = a \times (1-1) = a-a = 0 \\
\\
& (-1) \times (-1 + 1) = (-1)\times (-1) -1 = 0 \qquad \therefore(-1) \times (-1) = 1
\end{align}
$$

# Iterated Operations

 $a$ is a number in the number line; $n$ is a positive whole number

### multiplication
---
$$
\text{add } a \text{ n times:} \quad a+a+a+ \dots + a = a \times n
$$
### exponentiation
---
$$
\text{multiply } a \text{ n times:} \quad a \times a \times a \times \dots a = a^n \quad (\text{base: a} \quad \text{exponent: n})
$$

when n=1, their notations are excluded

# Number Systems
### rational numbers
---
$$ \frac{p}{q} \qquad p \text{ and } q \text{ are integers} \quad  q \neq 0 $$

**integers**
$$
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline \dots & -3 & -2 & -1 & 0 & +1 & +2 & +3 & \dots \\ \hline \end{array}
$$
$$
\begin{align}
& n \ \text{th digit} \implies a \times 10^{n-1} \qquad (a \text{ is integer, } 0 \leq a \leq 9, a \neq 0 \text{ if } n = 1 )
\end{align}
$$

**natural numbers**
$$
 \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline \dots & 0 & +1 & +2 & +3 & \dots \\ \hline \end{array} 
$$

### irrational numbers
---
$$ \text{numbers in the number line that is not rational} \quad (\text{e.g., } \pi = 3.141592 \dots ) $$

# Proportions

a, b are real numbers (b, d ≠ 0)
### equivalent notations
---
$$
\begin{align}
& a:b \qquad \frac{a}{b} \\
\\
& a:b = c:d \qquad ad = cb
\end{align}
$$
### circle
---
$$ \frac{\text{area}}{\text{radius}^2} = \frac{\text{circumference}}{\text{radius}} = \pi = 3.141592 \dots \qquad (\text{radius} > 0)$$

# Decimal System

terminating decimals / repeating decimals $\implies$ rational number
non-terminating & non-repeating decimals $\implies$ irrational number

### notation
---
$$
\begin{align}
& n \ \text{th decimal} \implies \frac{a}{10^{n-1}} \qquad (a \text{ is integer, } 0 \leq a \leq 9)
\end{align}
$$


### repeating decimal and fraction of integers
---

$$
\begin{align}
& X = a.n_1 n_2 \dots n_{k-p} \ \overline {n_{k+1-p} \dots n_k} \\
\\
& (10^k - 10^{k-p})  X = \text{integer} \implies X = \frac{p}{q} \quad \left( p, q \text{ are integers} , \ q \neq 0 \right)
\end{align}
$$
