# Prime Factorization

positive integer (>1) = product of prime numbers

### divisor (factor)
---
$$
\frac{\text{positive integer } (>1)}{\text{divisor}} = \text{integer}
$$
### prime number
---
has only 2 distinct divisors (1 and itself); not 1
### composite number
---
has more than 2 divisors; not 1


### number of divisors of a positive integer
---
each distinct prime factors & positive integer exponents

$$
\begin{align}
& \text{for } a = p_1 ^ {n_1} \ p_2 ^{n_2} \cdots \ p_k^{n_k} \implies \text{divisors \#: } (n_1+1)(n_2+1)\cdots(n_k +1)
\end{align}
$$


# Polynomial

polynomial: sum of each term
term: product of number (coefficient) & symbol

degree of term: exponent of symbol;  like terms: terms that have same degree
degree of polynomial: max degree of a term in polynomial

### notation
---
0-th degree polynomial $\implies a$
1-th degree polynomial $\Longrightarrow ax + b \quad (a \neq 0)$
2-th degree polynomial $\Longrightarrow ax^2 + bx +c \quad (a \neq 0)$
.
.
.
n-th degree polynomial $\implies a_n x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\dots + a_1 x + a_0 \quad (a_n \neq 0)$

equation that holds true for any allowed input value

---

# Linear Equation

$$
\begin{align}
& ax + b = 0 \\
& \text{left-hand side:} \quad ax+b \qquad \text{right-hand side:} \quad 0
\end{align}
$$
### solution
---
$$
ax = -b \qquad \therefore x = \frac{b}{a} \quad (a \neq 0)
$$

# Law of Exponents

a, m, n are real numbers (a>0)
$\times$ and $\cdot$ are interchangeable (multiplication symbol can be ommited)

$$
\begin{align}
& a^m a^n = a^{m+n}\\
\\
& (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \qquad (ab)^n = a^n b^n\\
\end{align}
$$
### use
$$
\begin{align}
& \frac{a^n}{a^n} = 1 \quad \therefore a^0 = 1 \\
\\
& \frac{1}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{b} = a^{-1} \cdot b^{-1} = (ab)^{-1} \quad \therefore \frac{1}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{b} = \frac{1}{ab}
\end{align}
$$
