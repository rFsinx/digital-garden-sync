# Prime Factorization

positive integer (>1) = product of prime numbers

### divisor (factor)
---
$$
\frac{\text{positive integer } (>1)}{\text{divisor}} = \text{integer}
$$
### prime number
---
has only 2 distinct divisors (1 and itself); not 1

### composite number
---
has more than 2 divisors; not 1

### number of divisors of a positive integer
---
$k$ is positive integer); $p_{k}$ is prime factor; $n_{k}$ is natural number

$$
\begin{align}
& a = p_1 ^ {n_1} \ p_2 ^{n_2} \cdots \ p_k^{n_k} \quad \implies \quad\text{number of divisors}: \ \boxed {(n_1+1)(n_2+1)\cdots(n_k +1)}
\end{align}
$$

# Polynomial

polynomial: sum of each term
term: product of number (coefficient) & symbol

degree of term: exponent of symbol;  like terms: terms that have same degree
degree of polynomial: max degree of a term in polynomial

### notation
---
0-th degree polynomial: $a$
1-th degree polynomial: $ax + b \quad (a \neq 0)$
2-th degree polynomial: $ax^2 + bx +c \quad (a \neq 0)$
.
.
.
n-th degree polynomial: $a_n x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\dots + a_1 x + a_0 \quad (a_n \neq 0)$

# Linear Equation

$$
\boxed {ax + b = 0}
$$
### solution
---
$$
ax = -b \qquad \therefore x = \frac{b}{a} \quad (a \neq 0)
$$
